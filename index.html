Does Money Buy Happiness?

This website explores the relationship between GDP per capita and happiness scores across countries.

Explore the Analysis

Introduction

GDP is often used to measure a country’s economic success, but it does not always show how happy or satisfied people are with their lives.

This project compares economic data with happiness scores to better understand whether wealth is truly the key to happiness.

Key Findings

Economic Growth

Countries with higher GDP often have better access to healthcare, education, and infrastructure.

Happiness Factors

Happiness depends on more than money. Social support, freedom, and trust also strongly influence well-being.

Main Insight

GDP and happiness are positively related, but economic wealth alone cannot fully explain life satisfaction.
